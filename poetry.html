Lyric Poetry Fragment

O. Skeat 13 - Thebes, Egypt - Late 1st-Early 2nd c. C.E.

An ostracon fragment (pot shard), which is too incomplete to fully understand the narrative or provide a translation. A portion can be made out where Dionysus addresses his followers, the Lenaiai. Pan with his reed-pipe is among Dionysus’ retinue.

For more information, please see the entry on APIS.

Tragedy with Musical Notation.

P.Mich.inv. 2958. Greek with vocal notation. – Karanis, Fayum, Egypt. – Mid 2nd c. C.E.

A musical text likely used for performance. The text is too fragmentary to translate, but it makes references to the return of the mythological figure Orestes. It is divided into two sections, the first being a dialogue between two figures and the second likely being a choral response. The notes in this text likely represent vocal notation.

For more information, please see the entry on APIS.

Aristophanes, Heroes

P.Mich.inv. 3690. Greek. – Unknown provenance, Egypt. – 2nd c. C.E.

This papyrus preserves a fragment of a comedy of Aristophanes that did not otherwise survive to modern times. The fragment depicts heroes presenting themselves as distributors of rewards for the virtuous and of punishments for the unrighteous.

For more information, please see the entry on APIS.

Lyric Poetry Fragment

P.Mich.inv. 4336. Greek. – Unknown provenance, Egypt. – 2nd c. B.C.

Fragment of a poem in choral iambic meter. The content of the poem describes the initiation of a poet into a poetic tradition. The poet, inspired by the muses, wants to overcome certain, undefined obstacles and promote a new type of poetic art. It contains an appeal to the muses, which announces the theme. The theme of the rest of the poem, however, is lost.

For more information, please see the entry on APIS.
